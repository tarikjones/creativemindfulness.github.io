Technology

Technology is a part of everything we do. While technology provides us with entertainment and make our lives easier, it may be a good idea to limit our usage once in a while.

In a study from the University of Maryland which banned all forms of technology for 24 hours, the participating students began experiencing withdrawal symptoms after only a few hours. It may sound silly, but if you tried a similar test, chances are you'd feel the same. One student went as far as comparing his dependency to that of a crack-cocaine addict.

So has this addiction turned us into a bunch of impassive grunts, indifferent to social integration and natural scenery? Do we no longer notice the color of the grass? Are we forever assigned to a Facebook generation that only cares about how cute their profile picture looks? Sadly, the evidence would suggest so.

Why we should limit technology usage

While it may be a little different, we can still function with less technology.

  1. Your socialising skills will improve.
  2. Attainment levels increase.
  3. Decreasing your screen time will spark greater creativity.
  4. Limiting your usage of technological products will improve your concentration..
  5. You'll become a more well-rounded individual.
  6. You'll see things you've never seen before.

How to limit your technology usage

At this point, you probably realize how distracting and limiting technology can be. It may be difficult to slow down your usage, but here are some ways that can help.

  1. Don’t take it in the bedroom.
  2. Don’t wake up with it.
  3. Set a technology curfew.
  4. Take days off.
  5. Limit your social media use.
  6. Stop counting likes and followers.
